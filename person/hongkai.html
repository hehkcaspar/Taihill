About Me

Hongkai was born in Guangzhou (广州), China.
I attended Zhixin High School, where I began to build my view on the world and life.
I completed my undergrad and was in PhD program at Sun Yat-Sen University, majoring in computational mathematics and HPC. However, I ended up mastering out because, at that time, China lacked sufficient applications running on HPC to fully realize its potential.
In 2014, I moved to the US to pursue a second master's degree in Management Science and Engineering, a joint program offered by engineering and business schools at Columbia University.
My educational background has taught me to view the world from three distinct perspectives: scientific, engineering, and business. This multifaceted approach has given me triple the fun in exploring and understanding the world.
During my second year at Columbia, I co-founded TalentJungle and continued working on it until 2016. Afterward, I moved to Boston to become an Associate and EIR at BAC Investment, where I co-founded Boston Innovation Growth.
Since 2018, I've been working with my partner, Sky Yu, to build Taihill Venture.

Nonprofit

As a long-time supporter of the Boston Ballet, I'm thrilled to serve on their Board of Advisors. It's a fantastic opportunity to bridge the worlds of technology and art, exploring how innovation can enhance the ballet's artistic expression and reach.

Vice President @ U.S.-China Health Summit

The U.S.-China Health Summit fosters global health knowledge exchange and collaboration among healthcare leaders from the US, China, and beyond. Established in 2011 at Harvard University, the Summit has hosted annual public health conferences and, since 2020, online seminars and reports addressing the COVID-19 pandemic. Though currently paused due to geopolitical challenges, the Summit remains dedicated to its mission of improving healthcare through international cooperation.
